3. THE CONCEPT BEING EXPLAINED:
The underlying concept here is the relationship between force, mass, and momentum. When a force acts on an object (like a ball), it causes the object to have momentum. Momentum equals mass times velocity ("P = mV"). If the mass of the object is larger, it will have more momentum for the same velocity.

When catching an object, if we don't change the rate at which the object slows down (i.e., keep the denominator small), the force on our hand will be greater, causing pain. By pulling our hand back, we increase the time over which the object slows down, reducing the rate of change of momentum and thus the force acting on us.
