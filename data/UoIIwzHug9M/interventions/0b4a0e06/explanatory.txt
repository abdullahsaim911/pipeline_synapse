2. WHAT THE TEACHER IS EXPLAINING:
Your teacher is showing these graphs to explain Newton's second law and how it relates to the concept of net force as a measure of the rate of change of momentum. The teacher uses these graphs to illustrate that the baseball, with more momentum than the ping-pong ball, will result in a greater net force when stopped or slowed down.

3. THE CONCEPT BEING EXPLAINED:
Newton's second law states that the net force acting on an object is equal to the mass of that object times its acceleration. In this context, the teacher focuses on the law's relationship with momentum and explains that net force can be thought of as the rate of change of momentum. This means that a larger momentum change per unit time indicates a greater net force acting on the object.

4. HOW THE VISUAL LINKS TO THE CONCEPT:
The graphs visually represent the change in momentum over time for both the baseball and ping-pong ball scenarios. The steeper curve for the baseball graph shows a larger momentum change, which corresponds to a greater net force according to Newton's second law. Similarly, the smaller arrow for the ping-pong ball graph represents less momentum change, resulting in a smaller net force.
